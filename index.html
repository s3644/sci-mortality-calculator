📖 Field Reference Guide — How to Fill Each Parameter ▶

Parameter How to Determine Example / Range
Age at Injury Patient's age in years at the time of spinal cord injury. Use the date of injury, not current age if years have passed. 18–90 years
Sex Biological sex as recorded in the medical record. Used for risk models that differ by sex (e.g., pregnancy-related outcomes apply only to female patients). Male / Female
AIS Grade ASIA Impairment Scale from the most recent ISNCSCI exam.
A = Complete (no motor/sensory in S4–S5). B = Sensory incomplete. C = Motor incomplete (<3/5 in >50% of key muscles). D = Motor incomplete (≥3/5). E = Normal.		 A / B / C / D / E
Neurological Level Most caudal segment with normal motor and sensory function bilaterally.
Cervical = C1–C8. Thoracic = T1–T12. Lumbar = L1–L5. Sacral = S1–S5.		 Cervical / Thoracic / Lumbar / Sacral
BMI Body Mass Index = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). Use most recent weight and height from the chart.
<18.5 = Underweight. 18.5–24.9 = Normal. 25–29.9 = Overweight. ≥30 = Obese.		 15–55 kg/m²
Complication Count Number of current active SCI-related conditions from this list: pressure ulcer, UTI, diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, arthritis, falls, bladder accidents, sleep disturbance, severe pain, pain interfering with work, poor health perception, re-hospitalization. Count only diagnosed conditions. 0–16
(0 = none)
FIM Motor Score Functional Independence Measure — Motor subscale. Sum of 13 motor items (eating, grooming, bathing, dressing upper/lower, toileting, bladder, bowel, bed/chair/wheelchair transfer, toilet transfer, tub/shower transfer, walk/wheelchair, stairs). Each item scored 1 (total assist) to 7 (complete independence). Higher = more independent. 13–91
(91 = fully independent)
Injury Etiology Cause of spinal cord injury.
Traumatic = MVA, falls, violence, sports, other external cause.
Non-Traumatic = Tumor, infection, vascular, degenerative, congenital, other medical cause.		 Traumatic / Non-Traumatic
Education Level Highest level of education completed at time of injury or most recent assessment. Used as a socioeconomic proxy in risk models. Less than HS / HS-GED / Some College / Bachelor's / Graduate
Marital Status Current marital status. Used as a social support proxy — married patients tend to have better outcomes due to caregiver availability. Single / Married / Divorced / Widowed
